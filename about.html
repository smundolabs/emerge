Our Journey

The Emerge Story

From a small community initiative to one of Maryland's most trusted support agencies — four decades of making a difference.

The Beginning of Our Legacy

The Articles of Incorporation are filed for the Maryland Homes for the Retarded on December 6th 1976

Establishing Leadership

  • Maryland Homes for the Retarded changes its name to Maryland Homes for the Handicapped. Rev. David Michel, Jack Merriman, and Charles Helm are the founding directors.
  • Jack Merriman convinces Baltimore City to give the Mayor’s Residence on Maryland Ave to Maryland Homes.

Astor Courts

Rev Michel and Charles Helms convinces St Marks Lutheran Church to use its endowment ($500,000) to purchase Astor Courts on 25th and St. Paul to create housing for people from Rosewood.

Program Expansion

  • Community Link is formed in 1982 as a subsidiary corporation of Maryland Homes to own and manage Astor Courts, to be managed by Charles Helms, a for-profit corporation that pays its directors
